About

Hello, I'm Haosheng Wang. Currently a CS Master Student at Brown University. I have a broad interest in Artificial Intelligence. My long term goal is to develop intelligent systems that can reason and think like human (and potentially beyond!). My main research interests include: 1) Long-term memory: how could we make intelligent systems have long term memory like us? How does human's long-term memory interact with our own knowledge representation and knowledge adaptation, and how can they provide insights for LLM generalization and adaptation? related fields: LLM adaptation, LLM generalization, Long-term memory system (both neural and symbol, interal and external) 2) Goal design: humans are not born with labeled data nor explicit reward function, but we learn to reason and behave well (and emotion), how could AI agents (e.g. LLMs) learn and develop their own self-evaluation metrics? how can we design certain goals to help them grow in the right direction? how can they learn to design their own goals when they have being more intelligent than people (or for them to be more intelligent than humans?)? related fields: continual learning, behavior cloning, self-supervised learning, goal design, adaptive goals 3) Abstraction, symbol, and embeding: neural networks use continuous embeddings (pretty similar to human brains), but use language (e.g. token, word) -- discrete symbols -- to communicate (and/or to reason, to learn, to represent knowledge). What does this suggest about the tradeoff between symbols and embeddings, in terms of computational efficiency, interpretability, scalability, expressiveness? How could abstraction play a role here? related fields: neural-symbolic systems, abstraction, hierarchical RL
